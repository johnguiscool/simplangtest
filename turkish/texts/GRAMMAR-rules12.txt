<h4>The Simple Past Tense 1/2</h4>

Turkish verbs end with the suffix <b>-mek</b> (<b>-mak</b>).  To conjugate the verb in the past tense, drop this suffix and replace it with the <b>-ti / -di</b> suffix according to the table below:
<p>
<b>yapmak</b> - to do
<p>

<table>
<tr>
<td><b>(ben) yaptım </b></td>
    <td>I did</td>
    <td><b>(biz) yaptık</b></td>
    <td>we did</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(sen) yaptın </b> </td>
<td>you did</td>
<td><b>(siz) yaptınız</b></td>
<td>you did (formal)</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(o) yaptı </b> </td>
<td>he did</td>
<td><b>(onlar) yaptılar</b></td>
<td>they did</td>
</tr>

</table>

<p>
To form the negative, add the negation suffix <b>-mi</b> directly before the <b>-ti / -di</b> suffix.

<p>
<table>
<tr>
<td><b>(ben) yapmadım </b></td>
    <td>I did not do</td>
    <td><b>(biz) yapmadık</b></td>
    <td>we did not do</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(sen) yapmadın </b> </td>
<td>you did not do</td>
<td><b>(siz) yapmadınız</b></td>
<td>you did not do (formal)</td>
</tr>

<tr>

<td>he did not do</td>

</tr>
