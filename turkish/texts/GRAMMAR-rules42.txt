<h4>The verb "to have": "I have, You have, He has, etc." </h4>

In Turkish, there is no verb "to have".  Instead, Turks use the word <b>var</b>, which means "exists".  To say that "I have a car," you literally say "my car exists".

<p>
<hr>
<p>
<b> In the Present Tense</b>

<p>
<b>Arabam var.</b> - I have a car. (literally, "my car exists")
<br>
<b>Kız kardeşin var.</b> - You have a sister. (literally, "your sister exists")

<hr>
<p>
<b>Past Tense</b>

<p>
<b>Arabam vardı.</b> - I had a car.
<br>
<b>Kız kardeşin vardı. </b> - You had a sister.
<hr>

<p>
<b>Future Tense</b>

<p>
<b>Arabam olacak.</b> - I will have a car.
<br>
<b>Kız kardeşin olacak. </b> You will have a sister.
