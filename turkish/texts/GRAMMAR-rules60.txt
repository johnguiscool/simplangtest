<h4>Temporal Adverbs</h4>

Temporal adverbs indicating when an action takes place typically occur at the beginning of a sentence in Turkish.

<p>
<b> Common Temporal Adverbs </b>
<p>
<table>
<tr>
<td><b>dün</td></b> <td>yesterday</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>bugün</td></b> <td>today</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>yarın</td></b> <td>tomorrow</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>şimdi</b></td><td>now</td>
</tr>

</table>

<p>

<b>Dün ne yaptın?</b> - What did you do yesterday?
<br>
<b>Bugün hangi kitabı okuyorsun?</b> - What book are you reading today?
<br>
<b>Yarın denize gideceğim.</b> - Tomorrow I will go to the sea.
