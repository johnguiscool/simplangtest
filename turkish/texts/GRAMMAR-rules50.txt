<h4>Indicative Sentences</h4>

Turkish sentences follow a [subject] [object] [verb] word order.

<p>
<b>John kahve içiyor.</b> - John is drinking coffee.
<b>Ben bir kalem arıyorum.</b> - I am looking for a pen.

<p>

Adverbs and prepositional phrases also typically come before the verb.  Turkish sentences typically always end with the verb in the last position.

<p>
<b>Ben İstanbul'da oturuyorum.</b> - I live in Istanbul.
<br>
<b>İki yıl önce Türkiye'ye geldim.</b> I came to Turkey two years ago.
<b>John, Mary ile konuşuyor.</b> John is speaking with Mary.

<p>

Typically, the subject of a sentences is omitted where it can be inferred from context, or from the conjugation of the verb.

<p>
<b>Seni seviyorum.</b> - I love you.
<br>
<b>Burada çalışıyoruz.</b> - We work here.
