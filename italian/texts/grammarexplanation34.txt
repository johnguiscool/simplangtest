The first person plural form of a verb in the present tense is generally formed by adding -iamo to the end of a verb.  This form can be used to express the indicative mood as in <b>Parliamo</b> : [We are talking] or the imperative mood as in <b>Parliamo</b> : [Let's talk].

<ul>
<li><b>Parliamo del futuro.</b><br>[We are talking about the future.] -or- [Let's talk about the future.] </li>
<li><b>Andiamo.</b><br>[We are going.] -or- [Let's go.]</li>
</ul>
