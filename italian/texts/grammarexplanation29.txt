Comparatives in Italian are easy to form.  To say that something has a quality to a greater degree, use the word <b>più</b>.  To say that something has a quality to a lesser degree, use the word <b>meno</b>.

<ul>
<li><b>più grande</b><br> bigger (more big)</li>
<li><b>più difficile</b><br> more difficult</li>
<li><b>più facile</b><br>easier (more easy)</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><b>meno grande</b><br>less big</li>
<li><b>meno difficile</b><br>less difficult</li>
<li><b>meno facile</b><br>less easy</li>
</ul>
