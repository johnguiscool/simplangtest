<h4>Practical Introduction to the Present Tense </h4>

Turkish verbs end with the suffix <b>-mek</b> (<b>-mak</b>).  To conjugate the verb in the present tense, drop this suffix and replace it with the <b>-iyor</b> suffix according to the table below:
<p>
<b>yapmak</b> - to do
<p>

<table>
<tr>
<td><b>(ben) yapiyorum </b></td>
    <td>I do</td>
    <td><b>(biz) yapiyoruz</b></td>
    <td>we do</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(sen) yapiyorsun </b> </td>
<td>you do</td>
<td><b>(siz) yapiyorsunuz</b></td>
<td>you do (formal)</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(o) yapiyor </b> </td>
<td>he does</td>
<td><b>(onlar) yapiyorlar</b></td>
<td>they do</td>
</tr>

</table>

<p>

There are some complications because there are actually two present tenses in Turkish, but following this rule, you will almost always me understood.  To learn more about the present tenses in Turkish, click <a href="grammar.php?grammarlesson=2">here</a>.
