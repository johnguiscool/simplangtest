The Italian words <b>di</b> and </b> have many forms and combine with the articles of the nouns that they modify.

<br>


Usage notes:
<ul>
<li> <b>di</b> means [of]. </li>
<li> <b>da</b> means [from]. </li>
</ul>


<b>da + il = dal</b> 
<ul>
<li><b>Da + il paese = Dal paese.</b>  From the town. </li>
</ul>

<b>da + al = dalla</b> 
<ul>
<li><b>Da + la chiesa = Dalla chiesa</b> From the church.</li>
</ul>
