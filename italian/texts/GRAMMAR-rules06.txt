<h4>Forming The Present Simple Tense 1/2</h4>

Turkish verbs end with the suffix <b>-mek</b> (<b>-mak</b>).  To conjugate the verb in the present tense, drop this suffix and replace it with the suffix <b>-r</b> according to the table below:
<p>
<b>yapmak</b> - to do
<p>

<table>
<tr>
<td><b>(ben) yaparım </b></td>
    <td>I do</td>
    <td><b>(biz) yaparız</b></td>
    <td>we do</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(sen) yaparsın </b> </td>
<td>you do</td>
<td><b>(siz) yaparsınız</b></td>
<td>you do (formal)</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(o) yapar </b> </td>
<td>he does</td>
<td><b>(onlar) yaparlar</b></td>
<td>they do</td>
</tr>

</table>

<p>
To form the negative, add the negation suffix <b>-mi</b> <i>instead of</i> the <b>-r</b> suffix.
<p>
<table>
<tr>
<td><b>(ben) yapmam </b></td>
    <td>I do not</td>
    <td><b>(biz) yapmayız</b></td>
    <td>we do not</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(sen) yapmazsın </b> </td>
<td>you do not</td>
<td><b>(siz) yapmazsınız</b></td>
<td>you do not (formal)</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td><b>(o) yapmaz </b> </td>
<td>he does not</td>
<td><b>(onlar) yapmazlar</b></td>
<td>they do not</td>
</tr>

</table>
